Human Immunodeficiency Virus

HIV infects white blood cells in the body's immune system called T-helper cells (or CD4 cells). The virus attaches itself to the T-helper cell; it then fuses with it, takes control of its DNA, replicates itself and releases more HIV into the blood. Our algorithm finds these viruses and gets a total count in a blood sample to return the intensity of HIV infection the patient has.
